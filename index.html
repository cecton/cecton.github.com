Profile

For the past several years, specialising in Rust to build everything from WebAssembly libraries to event-driven architectures, low-level IoT binary abstractions, and data modeling systems. Rust's combination of safety, performance, and expressiveness makes it possible to tackle problems that would be risky or inefficient in other languages.

Author of gptman and mbrman, both packaged in major Linux distributions like Fedora and NixOS — sometimes even used in their installers. Also a contributor to the Rust Yew framework. These started as tools needed for real work, but getting feedback from users discovering them in unexpected places is a constant reminder of why building software that others find useful matters.

AI has become essential to the workflow, but not as a crutch. Used for error detection, improving naming, exploring unfamiliar codebases, and handling tedious but straightforward tasks — amplifying productivity and code quality while keeping critical architectural decisions firmly human.

A Linux user since the age of 15, over 25 years, starting with Debian and Slackware, spending years on Arch, and now experimenting with NixOS. The terminal is where productivity peaks — scripting solutions rather than clicking through interfaces. On the infrastructure side, serverless architectures on AWS managed through Terraform, with a preference for Nix locally for reproducibility.

Over the course of a long career, the balance between engineering excellence and pragmatic delivery has become second nature. Large codebases and unfamiliar systems hold no fear — the goal is always code that is well-architected enough to evolve cleanly, but ships fast enough to deliver real value.
